Black Friday

Black Friday is the biggest shopping day of the year worldwide. Originating in the United States as the start of the holiday shopping season, it occurs the day after Thanksgiving. Now, people around the globe eagerly participate in this massive event.

Most stores offer significant discounts on Black Friday. Prices are slashed from 5 to 50 percent or even more, making it a prime opportunity for customers to purchase items at lower prices. The excitement over cheap deals often results in crowded stores, with occasional disputes over bargains (special deals).

Department stores, shopping centers, and supermarkets typically open their doors around 5:00 a.m., with some customers lining up overnight. Others opt for online shopping to avoid crowded places.

While many enjoy the thrill of shopping and finding bargains, it's important to be mindful of our purchases and not succumb to consumerism by acquiring unnecessary items.

Tips to Communicate Effectively in English

Students during these days are more conscious about the importance of learning a foreign language like English to get a better job, for business, for pleasure, for traveling or even for living in an English-speaking country. But they think it is difficult to develop linguistic skills to communicate correctly, so if you want to learn English, you may consider the following tips to be successful:

  • Don’t be afraid of making mistakes.
  • Enjoy listening to music and pay attention to the lyrics.
  • Watch movies, TV shows or news in English with subtitles and then, when you feel more comfortable, without them.
  • Record yourself speaking English. This exercise helps you improve your pronunciation.
  • Create a group with friends to talk and write using common vocabulary and expressions.
  • If you don’t understand something, ask someone: teacher, classmates, friends or check online.
  • And most importantly, make yourself a study plan and practice every day.

You can do so much better to communicate in English all the time if you follow these simple steps in your free time. Success is only one step ahead!
